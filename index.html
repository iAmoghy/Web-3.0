The New Web

Web 3.0

Welcome to the future of the internet, Web 3.0, where the user will become the owner of their data - not greedy cooperations, but you, the user.

Overview

Web 3.0 is essentially the third version of the internet. To better understand it is important to understand the two versions before it. Web 1.0 (the earliest version) is what is called a read-only web. This means that most people on the internet are consumers, while only the makers/web developers could make alterations. Web 2.0 is known as a read-write web. In this version you do not have to be a developer to participate in the formation of the internet universe. You can create things and share your thoughts with the rest of the world as seen on many social medias such as Facebook.

Web 3.0 is the latest version of the internet. It is known as read-write-execute. The difference between this and the previous version is that Web 2.0 was largely controlled by the tech giants such as Amazon or Microsoft, but Web 3.0 is a decentralised web, meaning people have control over their own data.
